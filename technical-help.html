Thread & Tension

Check that the thread is correctly seated in all guides and the tension discs. Ensure you are using the correct thread weight for your fabric and needle size. A bent or blunt needle can also cause thread breakage — replace the needle and re-thread the machine from the spool.

This is almost always caused by incorrect upper threading. Remove the thread completely and re-thread with the presser foot raised — this opens the tension discs and allows the thread to seat properly. Also check that the bobbin is wound evenly and inserted in the correct direction.

Uneven tension is usually caused by mismatched upper and bobbin thread weights, or a damaged tension spring. Start by adjusting the tension dial incrementally and testing on a scrap piece. If the issue persists, the bobbin tension may need to be recalibrated — contact our team for guidance.

Needle & Feed

A needle breaking is often caused by the needle being incorrectly inserted (flat side must face the correct direction per your model), using the wrong needle size for the fabric, or the fabric being pulled manually while sewing. Always let the feed dog move the fabric. Check the needle clamp is fully tightened.

First ensure the presser foot pressure is set correctly for your fabric weight — heavier fabrics require more pressure. Check that the feed dogs are in the raised position and are not clogged with lint. Clean the feed dog area with a brush and compressed air, then test again.

Skipped stitches on stretch or knit fabrics usually indicate the wrong needle type. Use a ballpoint or stretch needle for knit fabrics. On woven fabrics, a skipped stitch can mean the needle is dull or the timing is slightly off. Replace the needle first — it resolves the issue in most cases.

Motor & Noise

Unusual noise often indicates a lack of lubrication or a loose component. Apply machine oil to the designated lubrication points (refer to your model's manual) and run the machine at low speed for 2–3 minutes. If the noise persists, check that the needle plate screws and presser foot are fully secured.

For foot-pedal operated models, inspect the pedal connection and cable for wear. Clean the carbon contacts inside the pedal if accessible. For servo motor models, check the motor speed controller setting — the minimum speed threshold may need adjustment. Contact our team if recalibration is required.

Setup & Calibration

Ensure the thread is correctly guided through the bobbin winding tension guide before winding. Hold the thread end for the first few rotations to ensure a clean start. If the bobbin winds unevenly throughout, the bobbin winder tension spring may be worn and require replacement.

Verify the stitch length dial is fully engaged at your chosen setting and is not slipping. On older models, the regulator lever can become loose — tighten the locking screw beneath the dial. If the issue only occurs at high speed, the timing may need professional adjustment at a LOTUS service centre.

After a service, the machine timing and tensions are recalibrated to factory settings. If your previous settings were non-standard, re-adjust the tension and stitch settings to suit your specific thread and fabric combination. Allow a short run-in period of 15–20 minutes at moderate speed for the machine to settle.
